## 1. Project Background

The Course Confusion Radar Application is a web-based platform that allows students to anonymously submit confusing course topics after lectures or practical sessions.

Many students may feel embarrassed to ask questions in class. As a result, lecturers may not always know which topics students are struggling with. This application aims to collect anonymous confusion reports, allow students to vote “I’m confused too” on existing reports, and help lecturers identify common learning difficulties through a simple dashboard.

---

## 2. Target Users

The target users of this application are students who may feel confused about course topics after lectures or practical sessions but may not feel comfortable asking questions in class.

Lecturers are also important users because they can use the dashboard to identify the most confusing topics and adjust their teaching or revision focus.

---

## 3. Interview Method

For this practical task, target users were interviewed to collect feedback on their requirements for the Course Confusion Radar Application.

The interview focused on students’ learning difficulties, their willingness to ask questions in class, their opinions about anonymous feedback, and the features they would find useful in the application.

The collected feedback was then converted into software requirements and user stories.

---

## 4. Interview Questions

The following questions were used during the interview:

1. Do you sometimes feel confused about course topics after lectures or practical sessions?
2. Do you feel comfortable asking questions in class? Why or why not?
3. Would anonymous confusion reporting make it easier for you to ask questions?
4. Would you like to see whether other students are confused about the same topic?
5. Would voting “I’m confused too” be useful?
6. What information should students see in the system?
7. What information should lecturers see in the dashboard?
8. Are there any privacy concerns when submitting confusion reports?
